overview

One of the inspirations for our application was the multitude of studies on memory retention ability during exercise. After reading and cross-referencing numerous papers (all linked below), similar conclusions have been drawn: information retention is increased during light to moderate aerobic exercise. Adding to this, there are also studies about the positive correlation of spacing out memorization between different sessions. Taking these ideas and world exploration into account, we were inspired to create an application for memorizing foreign vocabulary while walking from place to place, aiming for better word retention.

Research Papers discussing the idea about light to moderate exercise while memorizing improving memory retainment:

  • Physical Exercise during Encoding Improves Vocabulary Learning in Young Female Adults
  • The Effects of Acute Physical Exercise on Memory, Peripheral BDNF, and Cortisol in Young Adults
  • Physical Activity Improves Learning a Second Language
  • Effectiveness of Studying When Coupled with Exercise-Induced Arousal
  • New Study Strengthens the Link Between Exercise and Memory
